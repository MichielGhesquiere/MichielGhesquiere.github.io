Motivation

My initial pursuit of a Master in Bioscience Engineering: Human Health Engineering at KU Leuven stemmed from a desire to integrate biological and engineering knowledge and apply this theory to real-world healthcare applications. To further deepen my understanding of AI and computer science, I am currently pursuing an advanced master's degree in Artificial Intelligence.

One particular area of interest for me is the department's research on wearable health monitoring. I have been involved in a Human Health thesis on physical activity recognition using wearables and am currently participating in a study for emotion detection, also based on wearables that continuously monitor daily activity and sleep. This experience has further motivated my interest in these topics.

The idea of fusing data from different modalities is quite captivating. This topic has piqued my interest and further motivated me to pursue a PhD in this field. The interdisciplinary collaboration between ESAT and UZ Leuven, where experts from different domains combine their expertise to generate valuable insights, is also highly appealing to me.
